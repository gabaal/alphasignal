### The Problem With the Fragmented Stack

The standard retail trader's setup isn't really a system. It's a collection of disconnected inputs that require *you* — the human — to do all the synthesis.

That's a problem because:

- **You introduce lag.** By the time you've checked five sources, the institutional money has already moved.
- **You introduce bias.** Twitter shapes your read before the data does.
- **You introduce fatigue.** Decision-making quality degrades across a 45-minute information-gathering ritual.
- **There's no structure.** You're not running a repeatable process. You're winging it every day and calling it a morning routine.

Professional quant desks don't work this way. They have a terminal. One interface. All the data, structured, prioritised, and actionable.
