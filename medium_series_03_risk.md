# The #1 Skill Nobody Teaches — Risk Management

## You don't need to be right more often. You need to lose less when you're wrong.

***

Here's a stat that'll change how you think about trading: you can be wrong on 60% of your trades and still be profitable.

How? By making your winners bigger than your losers.

This is the entire game of trading. Not finding the perfect entry. Not predicting the future. Just making sure that when you're right, you make more than you lose when you're wrong.

That's risk management. And almost nobody teaches it properly.

### The 1% Rule

Before you take any trade, you need to answer one question: **"How much am I willing to lose if this trade goes against me?"**

The professional answer: **no more than 1-2% of your total account on a single trade.**

If you have $10,000 in your account, you should never lose more than $100-200 on a single trade. Not "I think it'll probably be fine." Not "I'll move my stop if it gets close." $100-200. Maximum. Period.

This sounds overly cautious until you do the maths:

*   Lose 10% of your account → you need an 11% gain to get back to breakeven
*   Lose 25% → you need a 33% gain
*   Lose 50% → you need a 100% gain (doubling your money just to get back to where you started)

The maths of recovery is exponentially harder than the maths of loss. The 1% rule is how professionals survive the inevitable losing streaks.

### Position Sizing: The Formula Nobody Uses

Once you know your max risk ($100 on a $10K account), you can calculate exactly how much to buy:

**Position Size = Risk Amount ÷ Distance to Stop Loss**

Example:

*   Account: $10,000
*   Risk: 1% = $100
*   BTC price: $77,000
*   Your stop loss: $75,500 (2% below entry)
*   Distance to stop: $1,500

Position size = $100 ÷ $1,500 = 0.067 BTC (~$5,100 position)

Notice that your position size is determined by your stop loss, not by how much money you have or how "confident" you feel. Wider stop = smaller position. Tighter stop = bigger position. The risk stays the same.

### Take Profit Targets: The Risk-to-Reward Ratio

Now that you know where you're getting out if you're wrong, you need to decide where you're getting out if you're right.

The standard framework is the **Risk-to-Reward Ratio (R:R)**:

*   **1:1 R:R** — You risk $100 to make $100. You need to be right 50%+ to profit. Barely breakeven.
*   **1:2 R:R** — You risk $100 to make $200. You only need to be right 34% of the time. Now we're talking.
*   **1:3 R:R** — You risk $100 to make $300. You can be wrong 70% of the time and still profit.

**Never take a trade below 1:2 R:R.** If you can't find a logical profit target that's at least twice the distance of your stop loss, the trade isn't worth taking.

### The TP1 / TP2 Framework

At AlphaSignal, every signal comes with built-in take-profit targets and a stop loss:

*   **TP1 (Take Profit 1):** A conservative target — usually half the full target. When price hits TP1, you take partial profits and move your stop loss to breakeven. You've now eliminated all risk from the trade.
*   **TP2 (Take Profit 2):** The full target. Let the remaining position run to the full target.
*   **Stop Loss:** The invalidation point. If price hits this, the trade thesis was wrong. You exit. No questions.

This framework means that even if only 40% of signals hit TP2, the system stays profitable — because every signal that hits TP1 becomes a "free trade" where the remaining position is pure upside.

### The Psychology Trap

The hardest part of risk management isn't the maths — it's the emotions.

**Scenario 1:** You set a stop loss at -3%. Price drops to -2.8%, then reverses and goes to +15%. You feel like a genius.

**Scenario 2:** You set the same stop loss. Price drops to -3%, stops you out, then immediately reverses and goes to +15%. You feel like the market is specifically targeting you.

Both scenarios are part of the game. Scenario 2 will happen. Often. The temptation to "give it more room" or "remove the stop just this once" is the road to blowing up your account.

**The rule:** If you set a stop, honour it. Always. One "just this once" exception can undo months of disciplined trading.

### Never Risk More Than You Can Lose

This should be obvious, but it isn't: **never trade with money you need for rent, bills, or emergencies.** Trading capital is risk capital. If losing it would change your life in any material way, you're trading too large.

Start with an amount that you'd be disappointed to lose but that wouldn't affect your life. For many people, that's $500-2,000. Grow it with discipline. Don't add more until you've proven you can manage what you have.
