Your First 10 Minutes Every Morning: The AlphaSignal Daily Routine

A prescriptive five-step workflow. Exactly where to click, in what order, and what you are looking for at each step. Total time: under ten minutes before you touch a position.

Why sequence matters

Every piece of information in the terminal is more or less useful depending on what you already know when you look at it. A Z-score of 2.5 on a long signal means something very different in a confirmed Risk-On regime versus a Dislocation regime. Reading the signal first and the regime second means you are interpreting data without context.

The sequence below is designed so that each step narrows the decision space before the next step. By the time you reach individual signal cards, you already know the macro state, the institutional flow direction, and the top-ranked opportunities. The signal card is the last check, not the first.

1

AI Market Briefing — overnight context

Navigate to Macro Intelligence in the sidebar, then open Market Brief. The AI briefing runs every morning and covers five sections: Overnight Summary, Signal Environment, Macro Watch, Risk Signals, and Actionable Ideas. Read the Signal Environment section first. It tells you whether the overnight session produced any high-conviction signal clusters and what the dominant direction was. The Actionable Ideas section at the bottom lists specific tickers with the AI's reasoning for each.

You are not looking for trade ideas here. You are building a picture of what the market did while you were not watching and whether that changes your prior assumptions.

3 MIN OPEN MARKET BRIEF

The AI Market Briefing. Updated each morning before open. The Signal Environment section tells you whether overnight activity was bullish, bearish, or mixed. The Actionable Ideas section names specific tickers with confidence levels and reasoning.

2

Market Regime — which strategies are valid today

Still in Macro Intelligence, open Market Regime. The Hidden Markov Model classifier outputs one of three states: Risk-On, Compression, or Dislocation. Each state has a confidence percentage. If confidence is above 85%, treat the classification as reliable. If it is between 65% and 85%, strategies that work in either of two adjacent states are safer bets.

Risk-On favours momentum longs. Compression means range-bound strategies and tighter sizing. Dislocation means elevated volatility, correlated selloffs, and a strong prior for mean reversion rather than trend-following. Note the regime and hold it in mind for the rest of the sequence.

1 MIN OPEN REGIME VIEW

The Market Regime view. The current state and confidence level appear at the top. Below it, the regime history heatmap shows how frequently each regime has appeared over the past 90 days, giving you context for how unusual the current classification is.

3

ETF Flows — is institutional money moving in or out

Navigate to Macro Intelligence and open ETF Flows. This view shows daily net inflow and outflow data across all major Bitcoin spot ETFs: BlackRock IBIT, Fidelity FBTC, ARK ARKB, and the rest. Look at the last three trading days. Three consecutive days of net inflows above $200M signals structural institutional accumulation. Three consecutive outflow days signals distribution.

A single day of heavy inflow after a multi-day outflow period is not a reversal signal. It is noise until confirmed by a second day. The ETF flow data tells you what the regulated, institutional side of the market is doing with real capital, not what retail is speculating about.

2 MIN OPEN ETF FLOWS

The ETF Flows view. Each bar represents one trading day's net flow. Green bars are inflows, red bars are outflows. The cumulative flow line at the top shows the running total. Three consecutive green bars with rising cumulative flow is the setup you are looking for before adding exposure.

4

Alpha Score — ranked opportunity set

Navigate to Strategy & Backtester in the sidebar and open Alpha Score. This is a composite 0-to-100 ranking of 53 assets updated in real time, combining LSTM neural network output and XGBoost model agreement into a single score with a letter grade. Assets scoring above 80 with grade A and consensus HIGH are the ones both models agree on strongly.

Cross-reference the top five ranked assets against the regime from Step 2. In a Risk-On regime, high-scoring momentum assets are the correct direction. In a Dislocation regime, even a score of 95 with a LONG signal should be treated with more caution than usual, because the underlying regime is not supportive of sustained directional moves.

2 MIN OPEN ALPHA SCORE

The Alpha Score leaderboard. 53 assets scored from 0 to 100. The score bar colour shifts from yellow through green as conviction rises. Grade A means both the LSTM and XGBoost models agree. The WHY column gives a one-line explanation for why that asset scored where it did.

5

Live Signals — check your watchlist assets

Navigate to Live Signals. Use the category filter tabs (L1, DeFi, Exchange, Miners, etc.) to narrow to the sectors you trade. For any asset already in a position or under consideration, check the current Z-score and direction. If the signal has moved from LONG to NEUTRAL since yesterday, that is meaningful. If the Z-score is dropping from 2.5 toward 1.0, momentum is fading.

The ATR Stop shown on each signal card is the level where the signal is statistically invalidated. If the current price is already within one ATR of that stop, the risk-reward on the entry has compressed and the setup is weaker than it looks on the score alone.

2 MIN OPEN LIVE SIGNALS

Total time: nine minutes

Briefing: three minutes. Regime: one minute. ETF Flows: two minutes. Alpha Score: two minutes. Signal check: one minute. That is nine minutes of structured review that gives you macro context, institutional flow direction, model-ranked opportunity set, and execution-level signal data before you make any decision.

The mistake most users make is starting with Live Signals and working backward. That path leads to confirmation bias. You see a signal you like and then look for reasons to take it. The sequence above forces the opposite: you build the context first and then see whether the signal holds up against it.

What to do when the steps conflict

Sometimes the briefing is bullish, the regime is Compression, and ETF flows are flat. This is not a failure of the system. This is the system correctly representing uncertainty. When the steps conflict, reduce position size and wait for alignment. The clearest and most profitable setups come when the briefing, regime, ETF flows, and signal direction all point the same way. That alignment happens a few times a week, not every day.

When you have nothing actionable, the correct trade is to sit out. The terminal will still show you signals. Most of them will be noise in a conflicted environment. The ability to not trade is at least as valuable as knowing what to trade.
