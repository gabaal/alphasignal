Using the Macro Intelligence Hub: Regime, Flows, and Calendar

The Macro Hub is the widest view in the terminal. It covers the institutional capital flows, sector rotation, FOMC calendar, and regime classification that form the backdrop for every signal you read. This article explains what each tab does and when to use it.

What the Macro Hub covers

The Macro Intelligence Hub is accessible from the sidebar under "Macro Intelligence". It opens to the Market Briefing tab by default and contains seven tabs: Market Briefing, Macro Compass, Capital Flows, Sector Rotation, Capital Rotation, Correlation Matrix, Macro Calendar, and Market Regime.

These are not eight independent tools. They are eight views of the same question: what is the macro environment doing right now, and how is capital moving in response to it? The tabs build on each other, so reading them in order gives you a coherent macro picture rather than a collection of isolated data points.

The Macro Intelligence Hub. The tab bar at the top gives access to all seven views from one location. The Market Briefing is the starting point: an AI-generated summary of current macro context, key opportunities, and active risk warnings for BTC and the broader crypto market.

Market Regime: the first thing to check

The Market Regime tab runs a Hidden Markov Model against BTC price, volatility, and cross-asset correlation data. The output is one of three regime states with a confidence percentage. The Structural Alpha Heatmap below the main panel shows the regime history over the past 90 days as colour-coded columns: green for Risk-On, yellow for Compression, red for Dislocation.

The heatmap tells you two things beyond the current state. First, how long the current regime has been active. A regime that has persisted for 20 consecutive days is more entrenched than one that appeared yesterday. Second, how frequently each regime appears in this asset's recent history. If the asset has been in Dislocation for 60% of the past 90 days, the base rate of encountering a Dislocation regime is high and you should weight your strategy selection accordingly.

The three supplementary metrics below the regime label are worth reading every day: Trend Bias, Volatility, and SMA 20 Distance. Trend Bias tells you directional lean (Bullish, Bearish, or Neutral). Volatility tells you whether vol is elevated or suppressed. SMA 20 Distance tells you how far price is from its 20-day moving average, which gives you a sense of how extended or compressed the current move is.

Capital Flows: reading ETF institutional intent

The Capital Flows tab shows daily net inflow and outflow data across all major Bitcoin spot ETFs. The view used in the Article 2 daily routine lives here. The interpretation rules that matter most are directional consistency, not single-day magnitude.

Three consecutive days of net outflows from BlackRock IBIT signals that the largest institutional ETF holder is reducing exposure. This is a structural bearish signal for BTC, regardless of what the price is doing on any individual day. Single-day spikes in either direction are frequently noise: end-of-month rebalancing, tactical hedges, or authorised participant arbitrage.

The ETF Flows view under Capital Flows. Each bar is one trading day. The cumulative flow line at the top is the more useful metric for spotting sustained institutional direction over a week or longer. A rising cumulative line alongside a Risk-On regime is the clearest bullish macro alignment the terminal can show you.

Sector Rotation and Capital Rotation

The Sector Rotation tab breaks the signal universe into sectors: L1, DeFi, Exchange, Miners, AI, RWA, and others. It shows which sectors are currently attracting capital and which are seeing outflows, based on volume and on-chain flow data. This is the view to check when you want to know whether the top Alpha Score assets are concentrated in one sector or spread across the board.

Concentrated signal activity in one sector means a specific thesis is driving capital, not a broad market risk-on move. Distributed signal activity across three or more sectors means broad appetite for crypto risk. The latter tends to be more durable.

Capital Rotation overlays this with a cycle framework, showing where each sector sits in the rotation cycle relative to BTC dominance and altcoin beta. When L1s are leading and DeFi is lagging, the cycle is in early-stage altcoin expansion. When everything is outperforming BTC simultaneously, late-cycle exhaustion is more likely.

Macro Calendar: event risk you cannot ignore

The Macro Calendar tab lists upcoming macroeconomic events: FOMC meetings, CPI and NFP releases, Fed speaker appearances, and Treasury auctions. Each event is scored for its historical impact on BTC price over the 48 hours following the announcement. FOMC decisions carry a consistently high impact score. CPI prints are variable depending on whether the reading surprises relative to consensus.

The practical use of the calendar is simple: if you are considering entering a position within 24 hours of a high-impact event, reduce sizing by at least half. FOMC weeks have shown above-average volatility and correlation breakdown across crypto assets. The regime classifier often shifts during FOMC weeks. Entering with full position size before a FOMC meeting is accepting regime-change risk without compensation.

Correlation Matrix: when crypto stops trading like crypto

The Correlation Matrix shows the rolling 30-day correlation between BTC and traditional asset classes including equities (SPX), gold, DXY (US Dollar Index), and bond yields. In a normal Risk-On regime, BTC correlates positively with equities and negatively with DXY. During macro stress events, those correlations break down or invert temporarily.

When BTC-SPX correlation drops below 0.3 in a Dislocation regime, it means BTC is decoupling from the equity market risk-off trade. This is either idiosyncratic crypto stress (on-chain or exchange-related) or early institutional accumulation that is diverging from the broader macro risk appetite. Both require different responses.

The Correlation Matrix gives you early warning when the macro regime is changing in ways the HMM classifier has not yet caught. Correlations typically shift before the regime label changes. If you see BTC-DXY correlation flipping positive while the regime still shows Risk-On, the regime label is about to update. Trade that transition carefully.
